Career Life

Kate Abdo started her career in sports journalism back in 2003. She had early six-month work experience in Munich and a further six months in Paris.

She joined Deutsche Welle’s foreign news network later. Whereas, she served as an intern with the network’s Foreign Languages Editorial Team from September 2005 through September 2006.

Moreover, her job was to translate the stories from Spanish and German into English. Abdo slowly went up the career ladder and became the Deutsche Welle sports anchor.

Because of her English, German, and Spanish expertise, she was selected to host sports programs for her English and German services.

Abdo served as a production assistant for the ‘DFL’ from 2006 to 2007. She was responsible for producing reports in Spanish.

Abdo continued with Deutsche Welle as the sports anchor until June 2009. After leaving ‘DW,’ Abdo joined CNN International.

She also served as an anchor for the daily show World Sports. She has covered several CNN en Espanol sports events. Abdo remained with CNN until July 2011.

As its main presenter, Abdo joined Sky Sports News HD in August 2011. She has also been the public face of network launches.

Abdo was actively involved in establishing coverage of the network. She was the head of a team of 14 anchors and was very effective as a leader in her position.

In January 2014, Abdo moved to the UK to become a television presenter for Sky Sports. She presented ‘European Football, Transfer Deadline Day, and Sky Sports News pay-per-view boxing events.

In 2015, Sky Sports lent her to Fox Sports to host their coverage of the FIFA Women’s World Cup. Later, she accepted a permanent offer from Fox and transferred to the USA.

Since leaving DW, Abdo joined CNN International. She also served as an anchor for the daily show World Sports. She has covered several CNN en Espanol sports events. Abdo remained with ‘CNN’ until July 2011.

Abdo presented the Fox series Premier Boxing Champions. She was also seen on the daily studio program Inside PBC Boxing.

Whereas, she interacted in four languages at the 2014 ‘FIFA Ballon d’Or “ceremony. She has earned Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo the World’s Best Player award.
