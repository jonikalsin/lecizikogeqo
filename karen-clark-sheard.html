Karen Clark Sheard

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Friday, June 14, 2024

Karen Clark Sheard is a Grammy Award-winning gospel singer, musician, and songwriter from the United States. She is best known for being the youngest member of the Clark Sisters, an American gospel trio. She rose to stardom during the Clark Sisters’ sabbatical after recording her critically lauded and much-anticipated solo album Finally Karen, which included her smash “Balm in Gilead,” the R&B-flavored “Just For Me,” and “Nothing Without You.” You can learn more about her by reading the article below.

How old is Karen Clark?

Karen Valencia Clark was born in the year 1960 in Detroit, Michigan as Karen Valencia Clark. She is currently 59 years old. Reverend Elbert Clark and Dr. Mattie Moss Clark, a pioneering gospel choir director, are her parents. Her siblings are Leo, Jacky, Denise, Elbernita “Twinkie,” and Dorinda, and she is the youngest of six children. She is of Afro-American race and is of American nationality. Furthermore, she is a devout Christian.

She received her education at Mumford High School in Detroit, Michigan, where she graduated in 1979.

When a blood artery broke during hernia surgery in 2001, she was presented with a life-threatening situation. Due to her difficulties, her physicians gave her a 2% chance of life. She went into a coma after the blood clot was medically removed. She was in a coma for three and a half weeks, but she claims she recovered miraculously.
