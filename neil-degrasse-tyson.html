Table of Biography

Neil deGrasse Tyson is an American astrophysicist, author, and science communicator. He is popular for his work on NASA, and for publishing different books as well. He is a co-host of the series Star Talk.

Early Life

Neil deGrasse Tyson was born into the world on October 5, 1958, in New York City, New York, United States of America. As of 2023, he is 65 years old. His father’s name is Cyril deGrasse Tyson he was a Sociologist his mother’s name is Sunchita Maria Tyson she was a gerontologist.

He has two siblings named Stephen Joseph Tyson and Lynn Antipas Tyson. He belongs to Afro American Puerto Rican ethnicity and is known to be an atheist. His Zodiac sign is Libra.

Talking about his education he enrolled at the public schools in the Bronx. Later he graduated from The Bronx High School of Science. Moreover, the graduated with a BA in Physics from Harvard College. Later, he gets an MA degree in astronomy.

Moreover, he gets his MPhill degree in astrophysics and a Ph.D. degree in astrophysics respectively in 1989 and 1991.

Achievements

Neil deGrasse Tyson has won many awards throughout his career. Some of the major awards are the Medal of Excellence, Columbia University, New York City (2001), and the NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal (2004).

Other than this, he won the Science Writing Award(2005), the Isaac Asimov Award from the American Humanist Association (2009), the Dunlap Prize (2014), the Public Welfare Medal from the National Academy of Sciences, the Cosmos Award, the Planetary Society (2015).

Some of his other awards are Hubbard Medal, the National Geographic Society, Stephen Hawking Medal for Science Communication, Starmus, Grammy Award for Best Spoken Word Album nomination for Astrophysics for People in a Hurry (2017).

Professional Life

Career as Scientist

He started his career by researching cosmology, galactic astronomy, and subject-related astrology. He has held positions at the University of Maryland, Princeton University, and the American Museum of Natural History.

In 1994, he joined the Hayden Planetarium as a researcher while he was an exploration member at Princeton University.

As chief, he looks after the planetarium’s $210 million projects, which finished in 2000. In 2001, U.S. President George Bush appointed Tyson to serve on the Commission on the Future of the United States Aerospace Industry.

Likewise, in 2004 to serve on the President’s Commission on Implementation of United States Space Exploration Policy, the last also called the “Moon, Mars, and Beyond” commission.
