Kylee Russell- Biography

Kylee Russell, of Portuguese/Cape Verdean descent, was born on October 8, 1996, under the sign of Libra in Panorama City, California, USA. She is a 23-year-old actress best known for her roles as Karin Daniels in “Jump In!” and young Kari in “Mississippi Damned,” as well as Eliza in “Zombies.”

What is the Net Worth of Kylee Russell? Salary, Earnings

Kylee Russell’s net worth exceeds $500,000 as a result of her work as an actor in the film industry since 2005, where she has appeared in a number of TV shows and films. She has also become more successful as a vocalist.

Kylee Russell- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Kylee Russell grew up in Panorama City, California, where she was reared by her father, Norman T. Russell, and mother, Robin Tavares Russell. Kylee’s nationality is American, and she is of black ethnicity. She is a devout Christian.

Famous Siblings

Kylee is the middle of three children, with an older sister named Chelsea Royce Tavares and a younger sister named Halle Russell. Chelsea is also an actress who rose to prominence with her performances as Cranberry in the Nickelodeon sitcom “Unfabulous” and Autumn Williams in another Nickelodeon sitcom, “Just Jordan.” Halle, on the other hand, is a singer and songwriter.

Kylee Russell- Relationship, Boyfriend

Kylee Russell is romantically involved with a man named Samuel Houser, who works as a web designer. They began dating in 2016 and are still together – Kylee routinely posts images of them on her official Instagram account. Her present place of residence is Los Angeles, California. She adores animals and owns a puppy as a pet.

Kylee Russell- Professional Career

Kylee Russell made her television debut at the age of nine in the episode “Shelly And?” of the UPN sitcom “Eve” (2005), followed by guest-starring roles as Lola in the episode “Wake Up” of the NBC medical drama series “ER” (2005), and Trudy in the episode “Still Coaching” of the CBS sitcom “Still Standing” (2005). Rise to Fame in the Late 2000s

Kylee got the role of Karin Daniels in Paul Hoen’s comedy-drama film “Jump In!” in 2007, which was released on the Disney Channel. During the same year, she made her film debut as Roxanne Chambers in Ernst Gossner’s drama film “South Of Pico.” After appearing as Hannah in the episode “Let Sleeping Dogs Lie” of the Nickelodeon sitcom “Just Jordan” in 2008, Kylee rose to prominence for her depiction of young Kari in Tina Mabry’s 2009 drama film “Mississippi Damned,” which is based on her childhood.

By the end of the decade, she had also appeared in Ulli Lommel’s drama thriller “Absolute Evil – Final Exit” and the made-for-TV comedy “Kid Edition,” both in 2009.

The early to mid-2010s

Kylee first appeared as Briana in the pilot episode “There Goes The Neighborhood” of the Bounce TV sitcom “Family Time” in 2012, and her next part was as Mariah in the episode “As Long As We Both Shall Live” of the ABC Family drama series “Chasing Life.”

She next played Ruby in the 2015 drama film “Where Children Play,” written and directed by Leila Djansi, and appeared in Bill Duke’s documentary film “Light Girls” the same year. Kylee had a busy year in 2016 after landing the roles of Andy in the short comedy-drama “Code Red” and Sophia in the short comedy adventure “Virtual High,” followed by appearances as Hayden Chase in the made-for-TV comedy film “Hank Zipzer’s Christmas Catastrophe,” directed by Matt Bloom, and Jaiden in the made-for-TV comedy fantasy “Forever Boys.”

She also appeared on the 2016 EP “Hank Zipzer: Christmas Catastrophe (Music From The Original TV Series).” During the same year, she appeared as a guest star on TV shows such as “Girl Meets World” and “Modern Family.”

Recent and Future Projects

Kylee was cast as Penny Petzinger in Dan Samiljan’s short comedy horror “The Sub” in 2017, and then as Eliza in the Disney Channel Original Movie musical “Z-O-M-B-I-E-S” (2018) opposite Meg Donnelly and Milo Manheim.

Kylee was recently cast as Hazel in Camille Stochitch’s 2019 family film “Adventures Of Dally & Spanky.” It has been reported that she will reprise her role as Eliza in the upcoming sequel to the Disney Channel musical “Z-O-M-B-I-E-S,” titled “Z-O-M-B-I-E-S 2.”
