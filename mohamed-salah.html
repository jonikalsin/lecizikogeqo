Mohamed Salah - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Career, Facts

Posted by Martina Birk on Friday, July 19, 2024

Table of Biography

Mohamed Salah Hamed Mahrous Ghaly is a professional Egyptian football player. Mohamed Salah plays as a forward for Liverpool FC in the Premier League. He is known for his dribbling, speed, and unbelievable finish.

Early life

Mohamed Salah was born on 1992 June 15 in Nagrig, Basyoun, Egypt. Currently, he is 30 years old and follows the Muslim religion. He was born into a middle-class family, his family didn’t have a good financial condition.

Salah was a born football player. His academics weren’t very good which made his parents worry. Football always was his priority during his childhood and teenage.

He used to watch and learn football on TV. Salah used to watch his early idols and learn from them. His early idols were some Arabic players. He names  Zinedine Zidane, Ronaldo, and Francesco Totti as his childhood idols.

His parents always wanted him to focus on a conventional career. However, he was unaffected by his parent’s wishes. Salah kept himself completely grossing in football.

In his early days, he used to play football only for fun. Later, he joined the local youth club ‘El Mokawloon’ in Cairo and started to take football as a career.

His youth club was very far from his home, he needed to change 5 buses to reach there, which harmed his studies. However, Salah kept his passion for the game and focused on his football career.

Moreover, after a lot of hard work and passion for the game, he qualified for the senior team of his club. At the start, he used to play as a substitute but later, he became the regular forward of the team. Later, he got his nickname, Egyptian Messi.

Achievements

Throughout his career, he has scored many goals and has won many titles along with his team. Salah won the Swiss Super Cup with Basel in 2014. He has won the Premier League, UEFA Super Cup, Champions League, and Fifa Club  World Cup with Liverpool.

In 2017, He was able to win the Premier League Golden Boot with fifteen goals himself. Moreover, Salah came third as the best men’s player in 2018.

Mohamed Salah – Professional life

In 2012, the Egyptian premier league faced a shutdown due to which many players lost hope. Due to the shutdown, He was offered a contract by the Swiss club ‘Basel’. Later, he signed a contract with Basel for four years in 2012.

Salah made his Champions League and Europa League debut with Basel in the same year. He had a very good performance in the Europa League and led the team to the semifinals. Salah played a vital role in the Swiss Super League Championship.

The team finished as runners-up. Salah was also part of the Basel squad that won the ‘Uhren Cup’. Basel had a match against the well-known club, Chelsea, Salah played very well against Chelsea. which made the club interested in him.

Later, in 2014 he joined Chelsea. In February 2014 he debuted for Chelsea against ‘Newcastle United’.

Caption: Salah celebrating after scoring a goal in Champions League finals Source: Instagram

He scored his first goal for Chelsea against Arsenal. Salah was loaned by Chelsea to an Italian club ‘Fiorentina,’ the contract was vailed till the end of 2015. In August 2015, he signed a contract for AS Roma.

At the end of the first season with Roma, he was named the best player of the season. He was also the top scorer of the season with 15 goals.

Salah had signed a long-term contract with Roma. Salah was unable to reject an offer by Liverpool. In June 2017, Salah made a club-record contract with Liverpool for 50 million pounds.

He was also named the player of the month for August by the Liverpool fans. Salah scored his first Hat-trick with Liverpool against Watford. He scored 4 goals all by himself and led the team to a 5-0 victory.
